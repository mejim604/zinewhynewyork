Features

LINH AN

From: Hanoi, Vietnam.
2 years in New York City.


Fun Fact: “I love bubble tea and wine.. Not together though”

ABOUT LINH: During her time training at Broadway Dance Center in the International Student Visa Program, she was awarded the Outstanding Student Award, for her consistent diligence in learning and creating. Her choreographic work is largely influenced by the theater jazz style. She performed in the Royal Albert Hall in London and was one of the ten students chosen to perform in the Shanghai World Expo’s Opening Ceremony.

LIZA DE LA GARZA

From: Monterrey, Mexico.
3 years in New York City.


FUN FACT: “I learned to dance flamenco when I was 2 years old.”

ABOUT LIZA: She started training more commercial styles such as tap, ballet and contemporary, whichis her favorite dance style now. She is an Arts Management major at Pace University. She has performed in two Choreographer’s Carnival in NYC with Hayden J Frederick and Theresa Stone, with a special appearance by Todrick.

ROSE LU

From: Taichung, Taiwan.
6 years in New York City.


Fun Fact: “My nickname is Mulan because growing up I was the only girl in a break dance crew of boys”

ABOUT ROSE: She trained in breakdance for most of her teenage years, and after moving to New York City to get a degree in Film she took on different commercial styles, such as Street Jazz. She has danced live for the Kpop band BTS, and appeared in music videos for The Jonas Brothers and Daddy Yankee. She is also part of the New York based company: aKompany, directed by Kenichi Kasamatsu.

ELODIE DUFROUX

From: Loudun, France.
5 years in New York City.


Fun Fact: “I have two master’s degrees, one bachelor’s degree and a professional training and none of them are in dance”

ABOUT ELODIE While in NY, and due to her hard work and dedication to others in dance, she quickly became one of the most sought after assistants in the field. Her expertise led her to work directly with choreographers such as Hamilton’s Jon Rua, Stacie Webster, and Atlantic Records choreographer, Carlos Neto.

CARLOS NETO

From: Lisbon, Portugal.
6 years in New York City.


FUN FACT: “My first performance job as a kid was as a clown, mime and baloon sculptor. It is what ignited my love for the arts and performance.”

ABOUT CARLOS: He started dancing at 19 y/o. after moving to the UK to study journalism. Has worked personally with Simon Cowell, and did motion capture for Nintendo and PlayStation. He is currently teaches Street Jazz at Broadway Dance Center in NYC.
